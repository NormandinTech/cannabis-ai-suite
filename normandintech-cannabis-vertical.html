The problem

The industry runs on guesswork

Cannabis operators face regulatory chaos, hostile ad platforms, manual compliance, and tightening margins — all while a wave of debt matures in 2026. The businesses that survive will be the ones running on AI.

01
Compliance changes overnight
State regulations shift constantly with no warning. Florida's 64ER25-6, New York's billboard ban, FCC TCPA 2025 rules — most operators learn about changes after they're already in violation. Manual tracking doesn't scale across multiple states.
02
Ad platforms are hostile territory
Instagram, Google, and Facebook aggressively restrict cannabis advertising — shadow-banning accounts, flagging content, and disapproving campaigns. Operators trying to grow have almost no paid acquisition channels available to them.
03
Inventory is managed on gut feel
Most dispensaries know what sold last week, but not why. Overstock destroys margins. Stockouts destroy loyalty. A $30k wrong buy on the wrong strain can wipe out a month of profit — and it keeps happening manually everywhere.
04
Debt wave hitting in 2026
A major wave of cannabis debt is maturing in 2026. Operators need to extract significantly more profit from existing operations — without massive new capital investment. AI efficiency tools aren't optional anymore. They're the path to survival.
